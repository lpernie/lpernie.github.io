CERN Laboratory

I'm often located in Geneva, Switzerland where I conduct research at CERN laboratory (European Council for Nuclear Research). The CERN laboratory has 22 member states, and it is a dynamical environment where physicist from all the world collaborate together. CERN contains the Large Hadron Collider (LHC): the world's largest and most powerful particle accelerator. The LHC consists of a 27-kilometre ring of superconducting magnets and accelerating structures. From here, the beams are made to collide at four locations around the accelerator ring, corresponding to the four experiments: ATLAS, CMS, ALICE and LHCb.
I arrived at CERN to work in CMS for my undergraduate thesis in 2006, and I immediatly felt in love with the stimulating and enriching evnironment.

CMS Experiment at CERN

The Compact Muon Solenoid (CMS) is a general-purpose detector at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). It has a broad physics programme ranging from studying the Standard Model (including the Higgs boson) to searching for extra dimensions and particles that could make up dark matter.
Since I entered the CMS collaboration I worked both in the analyis of the large amount of data collcted by the experiment, and I also worked in detector performance, making sure the experiment is constantly at its best condition. In both cases I started as a student, and now lead goups is Ph.D. and postdoc towar long and short term plans, aimed to improve or add value to the data collected.
